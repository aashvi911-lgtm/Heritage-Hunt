Mandu

auto_stories The Story

Mandu was a medieval fortified city built atop a vast plateau, once serving as the capital of the Malwa Sultanate. Known for its grand palaces, elegant stepwells, gateways, and Afghan-era architecture, Mandu spread across the landscape rather than concentrating around a single monument.

stars Why It’s Special

Mandu’s scattered ruins demonstrate how historic cities adapted architecture to natural landscapes. By blending palaces, water systems, and fortifications into the plateau’s terrain, Mandu reflects climate-responsive planning that reduced heat, conserved water, and harmonized urban life with the environment.
